Plinth — the Firmament (Ctrl+G)

Look up: the Firmament

The old word for the night sky meant "the vault of heaven." Press Ctrl+G and your vault is drawn overhead — every note a star, every [[link]] a line between them. Daily notes burn amber. Broken links wait as dim, unborn stars until you write them. Drag a star, zoom the sky, click anything to open it.

Built on three refusals

Most note apps add. Plinth subtracts the things that get between you and your writing.

Zero Cloud

Your drive, not ours

Every file lives natively on your hard drive as standard Markdown. Nothing is uploaded. Nothing to breach.

Zero Plugins

Nothing to troubleshoot

Core features are baked in, so you never chase a broken community plugin or a config file again.

Zero Friction

Open, type, close

No setup, no onboarding, no accounts. Point it at a folder and start writing in seconds.

Everything you need, nothing you don't

The parts of a real notebook, without the weight.

▪Plain Markdown files in a folder you choose. Your notes outlive the app.
▪Daily notes created automatically, one keystroke away.
▪Wiki links with [[Note Name]] to connect ideas.
▪Backlinks panel showing what points at the note you're reading.
▪Ranked, typo-tolerant search across everything you've written.
▪Tag explorer for #tags, right in the sidebar.
▪The Firmament — your whole vault as a living star map (Ctrl+G).
▪Command palette to jump, create and act without the mouse (Ctrl+K).
▪Light and dark themes that remember your choice.
▪Export your vault to a zip anytime. Leave whenever you like.
